Privacy Policy

Effective Date: Sep 6, 2025

This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) sets forth the principles and practices of Trinos Strategy (“Company,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) regarding the collection, use, disclosure, and protection of personal data. This Policy applies to all individuals (“you,” “your,” or “data subject”) whose personal data we collect, whether through our website, LinkedIn page, lead generation forms, or in the course of providing our services.

1. Purpose and Scope

This Policy is adopted to comply with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”), the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”), and the Personal Information Protection Act of the Republic of Korea (“PIPA”). It governs all personal data collected and processed by Trinos Strategy in connection with our professional activities.

2. Definitions

  • “Personal Data” means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
  • “Processing” means any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, transfer, or deletion.
  • “Controller” means the entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.
  • “Processor” means any third party that processes personal data on behalf of the Controller.

3. Categories of Personal Data Collected

  1. Identification Data – name, title, company, position.
  2. Contact Data – email address, phone number, mailing address.
  3. Professional Data – business needs, areas of interest, prior interactions with us.
  4. Technical Data – IP address, browser type, device information, and usage logs.
  5. Other Data – any information voluntarily provided through forms, surveys, or communications.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Contractual necessity: to provide requested services or consultations.
  • Legitimate interests: to manage client relationships, develop business, and improve our services.
  • Consent: where you have affirmatively opted in to receive communications.
  • Legal obligation: where processing is required by applicable law.

5. Purposes of Processing

  • To respond to inquiries and provide services.
  • To conduct strategic advisory, mediation, and consulting engagements.
  • To manage business development, marketing, and client communications.
  • To comply with regulatory or legal obligations.
  • To protect the rights, property, or safety of our Company, clients, or third parties.

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell or rent personal data. We may disclose personal data only to:

  • Service Providers and Processors engaged under contractual obligations of confidentiality and data protection.
  • Professional Advisors (legal, accounting, technical) as reasonably required.
  • Regulatory Authorities or law enforcement, if legally compelled.
  • Affiliates or Successors in connection with business transfers or mergers.

7. International Data Transfers

As a cross-border consulting and dispute-prevention firm, we may transfer personal data internationally. Where such transfers occur, we implement appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), to ensure lawful processing and adequate protection.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described herein, or as required by applicable law, regulation, or professional obligations. Retention periods may vary depending on the type of data and jurisdiction.

9. Data Subject Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have the following rights:

  • Right of Access – to obtain confirmation of whether we process your data and receive a copy.
  • Right of Rectification – to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right of Erasure – to request deletion of personal data (“right to be forgotten”).
  • Right to Restrict Processing – to limit processing under certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability – to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object – to object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent – to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

Requests to exercise these rights may be submitted to dk@trinos.group.

10. Security Measures

We implement technical, organizational, and administrative measures to safeguard personal data against unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These include encryption, access controls, and staff training.
